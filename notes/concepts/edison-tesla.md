# Edison vs Tesla — Who Actually Changed the World More?

## Tesla — Legitimately Brilliant

- **AC power systems** — he was right and it mattered enormously. The entire modern grid runs on his work.
- **Induction motor** — elegant design, still dominant today
- **Early radio work** — Marconi basically used his patents
- Genuine intuition for electromagnetic phenomena that was ahead of his time

## Tesla — Genuinely Out There

- **Wardenclyffe Tower** — wanted to transmit power wirelessly through the earth. The physics doesn't work at useful scale. It wasn't just lack of funding that killed it.
- Claimed to have received signals from Mars
- Later life included death ray claims and increasingly unbuildable grand visions
- Obsessive behaviors, mystic numerology, the pigeon thing

**Pattern:** real intuition followed by overclaiming. He'd see something physically real — wireless energy transmission is possible in limited ways — and extrapolate way beyond what the physics supports.

---

## Edison — Legitimately Brilliant

- Phonograph was genuinely his and genuinely world-changing — recorded sound is a big deal
- Practical incandescent bulb + the entire electrical distribution system to power it — built the first real grid end-to-end in Manhattan
- Invented industrial R&D — the idea of a paid team systematically working on invention as a business

## Edison — Legitimately a Dick

- **The AC/DC war** — publicly electrocuted animals (including an elephant, Topsy) to demonstrate AC was dangerous. Knew he was wrong on the science, did it anyway for business reasons.
- Stole or took credit for work from lab employees who got nothing
- Screwed Tesla specifically — promised $50,000 to improve DC motors, Tesla did it, Edison said it was a joke and didn't pay
- Used patents aggressively to crush competitors — early film companies fled to California to escape his lawyers. That's literally why Hollywood is where it is.

---

## Who Changed the World More?

**Edison's DC grid was a dead end.** It worked in lower Manhattan for a while, then got replaced. He was on the wrong side of the most important electrical engineering decision in history, knew it, and fought it dishonestly anyway.

**The AC grid is foundational.** Every piece of modern civilization runs on it — the internet, hospitals, factories, refrigeration, computing. None of it exists without reliable long-distance power transmission. That's Tesla and Westinghouse.

The phonograph matters. Practical electric light matters. But "changed the world more" is a hard argument when one guy's core contribution got replaced and the other's is literally the infrastructure everything runs on.

The internet mythology turned Tesla into a martyred genius suppressed by evil capitalists, which is reductive. And Torvalds-style contrarian takes for Edison flatten the real story the other way. Both were genuine inventors. Edison was also a ruthless pragmatic liar. Tesla was also genuinely unhinged later in life. Both things are true.

## The Grid Today — A Behemoth That Needs Updating

The grid Tesla's work made possible is now one of the harder infrastructure problems humans face. It wasn't designed — it accumulated over a century.

**What it actually is:** Thousands of regional systems built by different utilities, governments, and companies, loosely interconnected. The US grid has ~450,000 miles of transmission lines, thousands of substations, and equipment averaging 40+ years old. Some transformers in service were built in the 1950s.

**Why it's hard to update:**

- **Can't take it offline** — unlike a server, the grid has to keep running while you work on it. Everything is interconnected so changes ripple unpredictably.
- **No single owner** — in the US it's a patchwork of private utilities, public utilities, cooperatives, and government entities. Coordinating upgrades across hundreds of organizations with different incentives is a political problem as much as an engineering one.
- **Transformer problem** — large high-voltage transformers cost millions each, take 1-2 years to manufacture (mostly overseas), and are custom built per installation. No warehouse of spares.
- **Regulation** — utilities are heavily regulated, meaning slow approval processes for any major change.

**The core technical mismatch:**

The old grid was designed for one-directional power flow — big central plants push power out to consumers. Renewables break that model. Solar and wind are distributed and intermittent, and they push power back into the grid in ways it wasn't built to handle. Fixing that requires smarter control systems, better storage, and upgraded transmission — none easy to retrofit.

**Why it's urgent:** Extreme weather is stressing it harder than it was designed for. Texas 2021 — a grid that worked fine under normal conditions failed catastrophically under unusual stress because there was no margin. That's what happens when aging infrastructure meets conditions outside its design envelope.
